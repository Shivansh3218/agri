About the project

Data with a purpose: dignity for the farmer.

KrishiSetu began with a simple question — if we actually listened to farmers at scale, what would the data tell us to do differently?

The mission

Bridging the gap between farmers and decision-makers

Across rural India, the people who grow our food are often invisible in the numbers that shape policy. KrishiSetu is a bridge (a setu) — carrying the lived reality of the farmer into a form that officers, NGOs and policymakers can see, sort and act on.

This platform is built on a real survey of 396 farmers across three districts, asking 42 questions each about yield, income, water, credit, climate and government support. Every answer is preserved, scored and made explorable.

What we measure

Six dimensions of farmer wellbeing

💰

Income & Costs

Whether farming sustains the household, and how heavily input costs weigh.

🌪

Climate & Water

Crop loss from climate change and the pressure on groundwater.

🏦

Schemes & Credit

Access to PMFBY, subsidies, MSP and formal versus informal credit.

🛒

Market Access

Reliance on middlemen versus mandis and direct-to-consumer sales.

🌱

Practices

Fertilizer patterns, organic adoption and adaptation strategies.

🗳

Sentiment

Satisfaction with support and the desire to stay in — or leave — farming.
