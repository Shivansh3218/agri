Key findings

What 396 farmers told us.

Ten headline signals from the field — the stress points that a spreadsheet alone would never surface.

💰 Income doesn't stretch

82% of farmers say their farming income is not enough to sustain the household — the single loudest signal in the dataset.

📉 Costs are crushing

94% report that input costs — labour, fertilizer, irrigation — are a serious burden on their farming.

🏦 Insurance on paper only

95% have heard of PMFBY, yet virtually none have ever actually availed crop insurance under it.

🛒 The middleman still wins

69% do not sell at MSP, and a large share still depend on local traders and middlemen at the farm gate.

💧 Water is running low

57% report groundwater depletion or water shortages for irrigation — a slow-moving crisis under borewell-heavy districts.

😢 A generation wants out

84% say they would shift away from farming if given a better opportunity — a stark verdict on the profession's future.

The chain reaction

High input costs meet stagnant selling prices (no MSP, reliance on middlemen). Households turn to borrowing, often from moneylenders. Add climate-driven crop loss and groundwater stress, and the result is an income that never sustains — and a widespread wish to leave farming altogether. KrishiSetu's vulnerability score captures exactly this compounding stress, farmer by farmer.

Methodology

How the vulnerability score works

Each farmer receives a 0–100 score built from ten weighted stress indicators — insufficient income, burdensome input costs, poor government-support satisfaction, groundwater depletion, climate crop loss, no MSP access, middleman reliance, borrowing, loan default and yield dissatisfaction. Scores are banded: Low Moderate High Severe.
